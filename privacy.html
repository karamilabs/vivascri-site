Privacy

Your work stays on your computer.

Vivascri is a local, offline writing app. It runs on your machine, and your documents and your PDF library never leave it. This page explains, plainly, what that means and the few places anything is collected at all.

The app

  • No account. The free app needs no sign-up and no login.
  • Nothing is uploaded. Your drafts, your PDFs, the AI drafting, and the grounded citations all run on your machine. There is no server that sees your writing.
  • No telemetry. The app does not phone home, track usage, or send analytics.
  • Optional web fetch, only when you ask. If you paste a DOI, arXiv link, or URL to add a paper, the app requests that one open-access source (and, for citation lookups you initiate, the public metadata for it). Nothing about your document is sent — only the identifier you typed.
  • Optional cloud tiers (coming soon). Paid features like hosted inference, sync, and collaboration are online services and would need an account and a connection. They are opt-in, clearly labelled, and off unless you choose them. The free app never uses them.

This website

  • Privacy-respecting analytics, or none. If this site uses analytics at all, it is a cookieless, no-personal-data measure of page views. No Google Analytics, no ad trackers.
  • Fonts are self-hosted. The site serves its own fonts, so visiting it does not send your IP to a third-party font CDN.
  • Email, only if you give it. If you enter your email to be notified about macOS/Linux or updates, it is used only to send those updates. Nothing else.
